Vice-Chancellor

Professor Jason Scott MBE, became Vice-Chancellor of Arkham University on 1 January 2023.Prior to assuming his role as Provost, Professor Jason served as the first male Dean of the Faculty of Law at Arkham University. As Dean, he led curriculum change, revised the admissions process and created the Asper Centre for Constitutional Rights and the Centre for the Legal Profession, among many other initiatives. His strong relationships with students, alumni and faculty led to his successful leadership of a $53 million campaign for the Faculty’s new Jackman Law Building.
Provost Jason has brought to Arkham College his commitment to students. In 2016, he released the College’s new Strategic Plan: People, Program and Place, which builds upon the College’s reputation for academic excellence and interdisciplinary innovation and focuses on enhancing the whole student experience in order to make the College an even a better place to live, work and learn. As Provost he has emphasized the student experience with initiatives ranging from mental health and wellness, to revitalized food services, to student services and program enhancements, to infrastructure renewal.
Provost Jason teaches private and public law, supervises graduate students, and publishes extensively. He is a frequent guest lecturer and an active member of a number of committees both inside and outside Arkham University.
Provost Jason obtained a BA (1980, English and Sociology) and B.Ed. (1981) at the University of British Columbia and taught secondary school in northern British Columbia before attending law school. He completed his LL.B. at McGill University (1990), his LL.M. at the University of Michigan (1992) and her S.J.D. at the University of Toronto (1999). In addition to numerous other awards, in 2012 Provost Jason received a YMCA Men of Distinction Award
