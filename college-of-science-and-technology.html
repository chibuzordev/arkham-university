Philosophy, Mission, Vision

    The High-points of COST philosophical consideration and objectives, which guide the teaching of the theory and practice of science in COST, are:

  1. To give students an initial broad training in the core science subjects of the medical practice
  2. To give adequate opportunity for skill acquisition in the use of the surgical tools of science as an empirical field of study with the purpose of developing competence in and enthusiasm for the application of science and technology to solution of relevant societal problems.
  3. To allow the exercise of freedom of choice and promote self-realization and actualization. COST curricula have significant flexibility that allows students to conveniently change programme in similar discipline at the end of 100 and 200 levels of study.
  4. To promote inter-disciplinary orientation and make application of science to problem solving the goal of the training of the scientists. Entrepreneurship courses in the College curricula offer the opportunity for multi-disciplinary projects, which are designed to replicate private productive sector milieu and focus on social relevance, social acceptability and service to society.
  5. To inculcate in our students the core behavioural attributes- of probity, hard work, and commitment- of natural scientists in empirical data-gathering and discipline in data analysis and impeccable integrity in inferential decision making.

The vision of the College of Science & Technology is ;
To provide science education which empowers her graduates to contribute effectively to the advancement of the frontier of scientific knowledge and its application to solving societal technology problems.

To fulfill the COST VISION, the College is focussed on the Mission:
To provide wholesome and comprehensive science education that stimulates intellectual enquiry and engenders a quest for solving developmental problem in the drive towards service and leadership roles in the ingenuous and global societies
